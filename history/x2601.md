- [La Cuba de Life](https://historiamexicana.colmex.mx/index.php/RHM/article/view/4812/5485)

Few subjects are as controversial—or as thoroughly permeated by ideology—as the Cuban Revolution. Narrated and interpreted by both its apologists and its detractors, this event demands to be situated within a historical framework that allows us to broadly discern the coordinates from which these various narratives are constructed. In the case of the present volume, we are confronted with what might be described as the story of a love affair—one defined initially by hope and idealism, only to be brought to a close by disillusionment. During the height of the Cold War—at a time when the U.S. government commanded a vast media apparatus (including *Life* magazine) to pave the way for the ouster of Jacobo Árbenz’s administration, and to orchestrate a vicious media campaign demonizing that liberal regime—that very same magazine, *Life*, allowed itself to be captivated by Fidel and the Cuban revolutionaries. Indeed, it played a pivotal role in forging the mythical figure of Fidel Castro. Such is the scope of the story recounted in this study. In stark contrast to the tragic events unfolding in Guatemala in the mid-1950s, just a few years later *Life* embraced the insurrection of the *barbudos* with enthusiasm, chronicling their struggle with deep empathy right up until their ultimate triumph in early 1959.
