
Sunday January 25, 2026

We got up kind of early and the sun was blasting in our window so we decided
to have our breakfast of banana and rice cakes and gf bread and we were off
for our journey downtown.

We walked down Rio Lerma and then crossed Sullivan and headed up Serapio Rendón
and then crossed over Insurgentes to the Monumento a la Revolución.  It was
our first time there and it just so happened this was going on...

Dia de la Bombera

So we got to watch the parade for awhile and close our ears as the sirens
were blaring all over the place.  So that was fun.

From there we headed over to the Diego Rivera Museum.  It was amazing.

[Sueño de una Tarde Dominical en la Alameda Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sue%C3%B1o_de_una_Tarde_Dominical_en_la_Alameda_Central)

We were there for awhile, listened to a tour group in spanish and took lots of
pictures.  What a place.

We walked across the Alameda Park and then started thinking about lunch.  We
stopped off at a crappy restaurant that was recommended and then the star of
the show was Pasteleria Ideal.  We got two rounds of food.  The first time we
bought two cookies and then we went back for another round of two cookies.  The
first time we sat down on the window seal and ate it there and the second time
we took the cookies with us.

From there we walked over to the subway station Salto del Agua.  It was our
first time entering the subway at that stop.  Last weekend we entered at
Merced and of course we have rode the bus from Balderas many times.

We got home, unpacked our cookies and other stuff and then walked over to
Walmart to get our dinner salad along with some other stuff mainly bananas,
an avacado and rice cakes.  We came home, Hb took a shower and then we had
our yummy dinner of salad and the cakes from the Pasteleria.

An amazing Sunday in Mexico City was had by all.

El Día de la Bombera y el Bombero en la CDMX ya tiene fecha oficial y se ha convertido en una jornada para reconocer la labor de una de las instituciones más queridas de la capital.

Cada 25 de enero, la capital rinde homenaje al Heroico Cuerpo de Bomberos, destacando su papel fundamental en la protección de la población, la atención de emergencias y el rescate de personas y seres sintientes.

Este 2026, la conmemoración cobra un significado especial al celebrarse por primera vez de manera oficial, junto con el 170 aniversario del Heroico Cuerpo de Bomberos de la CDMX, con actividades públicas como un desfile en el Centro Histórico.

La fecha no solo recuerda el origen histórico de esta corporación, sino que también visibiliza el trabajo de las mujeres bomberas y refuerza el reconocimiento social a quienes arriesgan su vida todos los días por la ciudad.
